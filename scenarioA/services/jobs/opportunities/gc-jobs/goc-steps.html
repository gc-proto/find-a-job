Timeline for the hiring process

The hiring process for the government can take a long time. Once you have applied, keep in mind the closing date listed on the job posting. This date will let you know when that department or agency has stopped accepting applications; only then will the interviews and assessments begin. The assessment and interview processes are lengthy. On average, each government job posting recieves XX applications and going through each of them takes time. If you do not hear back right away after you apply or finish an assessment, do not be discouraged. Things move slowly because the government is committed to finding the perfect candiate for the job

What is a pool?

Once you have passed the assessments and interviews, you will be placed in a pool with other, qaulified candidates. As the hiring process continues, the pool gets smaller until one candidate remains. This is the successful candidate. Once in a pool, you are in that pool for 6 months to a year. This means that employers in the goverment can pull from that pool of candidates who have be screened already. Even if you are not successful in getting the job you applied for, getting into a pool may open doors for other jobs in the government.

Types of employment

There are several ways you can get hired with the federal government. Permanent employment with the government is called "indetermindate" whereas shorter term employment is known as either a "casual" or a "term" position.
